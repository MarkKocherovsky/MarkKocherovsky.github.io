Recommended Readings

The following are books, articles, and reading lists that I recommend. Most of them are about socialist theory, and none of them are my own writing.

Obviously, this page is under constant construction and re-construction. I've linked you to the legitimate sources to shield myself from consequences but please use LibGen before paying for anything.

Anarchism

Truth be told, I recommend Zoe Baker's Reading List for Anarchist, Marxist, and Feminist Theory, but I'll try and put together a short list of key introductions and books I recommend, in hopefully chronological order.

Classical Introductory Texts

  • Stirner, M. (1995). Stirner: The Ego and Its Own. (n.p.): Cambridge University Press. Available on The Anarchist Library; (Different translations might use Unique for Ego and/or his for its)
  • Stirner, M. (2012). Stirner's Critics. United States: LBC Books. Available on The Anarchist Library
  • Malatesta, E. (2022). Anarchy. Czechia: DigiCat.Available on The Anarchist Library
  • Kropotkin, P. (1892). The Conquest of Bread. Available on The Anarchist Library
  • Kropotkin, P., Robinson, V. (2020). Modern Science and Anarchism. (n.p.): Read Books. Available on The Anarchist Library
  • Leval, G. (1975). Collectives in the Spanish Revolution. London: Freedom Press. Available on The Anarchist Library

Modern Introductory Texts

  • Bookchin, M. (1990). The Ecology of Freedom. United States: Black Rose Books. Available on The Anarchist Library
  • Black, B. (1997). Anarchy After Leftism. United States: C.A.L. Press. Available on The Anarchist Library
  • Bookchin, M. (2004). Post-scarcity anarchism. United Kingdom: AK Press. Available on The Anarchist Library
  • Graeber, D., Wengrow, D. (2021). The Dawn of Everything: A New History of Humanity. United States: Farrar, Straus and Giroux.
  • Baker, Z. (2023). Means and Ends: The Revolutionary Practice of Anarchism in Europe and the United States. AK Press.
